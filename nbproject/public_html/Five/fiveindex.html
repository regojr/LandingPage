Reflections on Interruptions in Group Discussion

September 20, 2014 by Ryan

This is a good little study and highlights some solid occurrences.

But… There is a difference between what actually happens and what should happen. There is reality and the ideal and with enough practice the ideals can become reality.

I do not discount that this is reality. I was just at a meeting similar to the one described (3 men, 3 women) and saw variations of this happen first hand with likely the same percentages above. Then I saw a woman speak up and say “don’t interrupt or talk over people! We have to have good conversation or else we will never accomplish what we need to and I shut down when it happens.” What followed was that interruptions across the board by both sexes decreased dramatically and when anyone interrupted they apologized (shortly after recognizing that they had interrupted).

What happened in the beginning was exactly what was described above but when the women interjected she set an ideal state. She raised awareness that everyone was interrupting and that it was dysfunctional rather than functional. Under the new found awareness that the interruptions were negative everyone monitored more closely when they were interrupting each other and if they did interrupt apologized for it.

What I am suggesting with this story is women may advance more when they interrupt but overall that is increasing shitty behavior across the board (that is like increasing the amount of subprime loans you are doing instead of stopping them entirely). We need to decrease interruptions across the board and this will be a non issue. Furthermore as boards and executive teams become more diverse I hope to see less overall interruptions, not more, and that change led by women who in my view are more self aware than men. Thus my hypothesis is that interruptions is rooted in awareness of oneself and people interrupt because they cannot contain their impulses.

A great response from my friend Roanne:

Yes, I agree.

I kept thinking that this article was encouraging behavior in a Prisoner’s dilemma sort of way rather than lifting all boats in the water. We’ve had discussions and I think you’ve definitely impacted me in being more self-aware and shifting away from my aggressive tendencies in a dog eats dog type of mentality. Ironically this article made me think that perhaps these recent personal development goals might hurt me because I (arguably) am not the optimal candid for this type of self improvement. Predominantly men need to increase their self-awareness and shift away from leveraging their power (both in formal organization structure and casual society) to domineer conversation. Or else my shift to be more mindful, listen before I speak, seek to learn before seeking to be heard, will have an overall negative impact on my career. In some ways I think that women don’t have the luxury to be mindful and listen before they speak because on the spectrum we already do too much of it and it’s not valued and “our fault” and instead told to “lean in.” If as a society, we want to raise all the boats and reach towards an optimal way to have conversations, it’s something women are already doing and need to be valued more for. Whereas men need to become more aware and shift accordingly. Or else you’ll get books published about how women just need to be more like men because men’s qualities are optimal and that’s why they succeed and we don’t.
